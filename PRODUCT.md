## Users

Two co-equal roles:

- **Comercio** (retail shop owners): small business operators placing repeat wholesale orders from their phone. Time-pressured, non-technical, mobile-first. Their job: find what they need fast, confirm an order, get back to running the shop.
- **Distribuidora** (wholesale distributors): manage product catalogs, delivery zones, and order fulfillment. More desktop-tolerant but also mobile. Their job: have full visibility over incoming orders and maintain their catalog without friction.
- **Admin** (platform operator): secondary internal role overseeing distribuidoras, comercios, commissions, and ratings. Desktop-primary.

## Product Purpose

Stockia is a B2B marketplace PWA that digitizes the wholesale ordering chain in Argentina. Comercios discover and order from distribuidoras; distribuidoras manage their catalog, zones, and fulfillment. The platform takes a 1.5% commission on delivered orders. Success means both sides abandon phone/WhatsApp orders in favor of Stockia.

## Brand Personality

Bold, sharp, trustworthy. References: Linear and Raycast — opinionated tools that feel premium without being showy. Fast, high-information-density, confident in every interaction. The UI should feel like it was built by people who care deeply about craft, not assembled from a component kit.

## Anti-references

- **Consumer marketplaces** (MercadoLibre, Amazon): colorful, promotional, badge-heavy, chaotic. Stockia is B2B — not a bazaar.
- **Clunky ERP software**: dense gray tables, overwhelming forms, zero visual hierarchy. The old-world B2B experience Stockia is replacing.

## Design Principles

1. **Tool confidence over decoration**: every element earns its place by serving a workflow. No flourishes for their own sake.
2. **Information density done right**: pack what's needed, nothing more. High density should feel organized, not overwhelming.
3. **Role clarity**: the interface should make each user's current context immediately obvious — comercio, distribuidora, or admin — without relying on onboarding.
4. **Mobile-first for buyers, responsive for sellers**: comercio flows are thumb-reachable and swipe-friendly; distribuidora flows can use more surface area.
5. **Brand without noise**: the Navy + Lima palette is the personality. Use it deliberately; don't dilute it with competing colors.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Adequate color contrast on all interactive elements, keyboard navigation, screen reader labels on icons and inputs. Reduced motion: respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for transitions and animations.
